Career pathways

Attending Burnley College's BPS division courses can be a great way for 16-17 year olds to explore and expand their career opportunities. The college is located close to many successful companies such as North West Aerospace, BAE, and Jet2, providing excellent networking and potential job prospects.

For students interested in pursuing a career in business, Burnley College's BPS division offers Level 2 and Level 3 courses in areas such as business and administration, customer service, and leadership and management. These courses can provide students with the necessary skills and knowledge to work in various entry-level positions in the business world, such as office administrators, customer service representatives, or team leaders.

If students are interested in pursuing a career in computer science, Burnley College's BPS division also offers Level 2 and Level 3 courses in areas such as IT user skills, software development, and network engineering. These courses can provide students with the necessary skills and knowledge to work in entry-level positions in the technology industry, such as IT support technicians, software testers, or network technicians.

Similarly, Burnley College's BPS division offers Level 2 and Level 3 courses in travel and tourism, which can provide students with the necessary skills and knowledge to work in various entry-level positions in the industry, such as travel agents, tour operators, or hospitality staff. These courses cover topics such as customer service, travel and tourism operations, and resort representatives.

In summary, Burnley College's BPS division courses can provide a great starting point for 16-17 year olds looking to explore and pursue careers in business, computer science, or travel and tourism. The range of Level 2 and Level 3 courses on offer can equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in their chosen field and potentially progress to higher education or apprenticeships.

School Leavers (16-19)

GCSEs

GCSEs are the qualifications taken by most students aged 14-16 in the UK. They are designed to test students' knowledge in a wide range of subjects and are often a requirement for further education or employment opportunities. Some of the positives and negatives of GCSEs include:

Positives: GCSEs provide a solid foundation of knowledge across multiple subject areas and are widely recognized by employers and higher education institutions. They can help students develop transferable skills such as problem-solving, critical thinking, and time management.

Negatives: GCSEs can be stressful and demanding, requiring a significant amount of time and effort to prepare for. Students may struggle to balance their workload with other commitments, such as part-time jobs or extracurricular activities. In addition, the grading system can be complex, making it difficult for students to understand their performance.

A-levels

A-levels are academic qualifications taken by students aged 16-19 in the UK. They are typically required for entry into university and provide a more in-depth study of specific subject areas. Some of the positives and negatives of A-levels include:

Positives: A-levels allow students to specialize in specific subject areas and develop a deeper understanding of their chosen subjects. They are highly valued by universities and employers, and can open up a range of career opportunities. A-levels can also help students develop independent learning skills and self-discipline.

Negatives: A-levels can be challenging and require a significant amount of independent study and preparation. Students may find it difficult to balance their workload with other commitments, and may struggle with the pressure of achieving high grades. In addition, A-levels can be expensive, with students often required to purchase textbooks and pay for exam fees.

Btecs

Btecs are vocational qualifications taken by students aged 16-19 in the UK. They provide a more practical and work-related approach to learning, with a focus on developing specific skills and knowledge for a particular career or industry. Some of the positives and negatives of Btecs include:

Positives: Btecs provide students with practical skills and knowledge that are directly applicable to their chosen career path. They offer a more hands-on approach to learning, which can be particularly beneficial for students who struggle with traditional academic subjects. Btecs are also recognized by employers and can lead to a range of career opportunities.

Negatives: Btecs may not be widely recognized by universities, particularly for entry into competitive degree programs. They can also be limiting in terms of subject choices, as they are often focused on specific vocational areas. Additionally, some students may find that the assessment methods used in Btecs, such as coursework and practical assessments, are more challenging than traditional exams.

Apprenticeships (16+)

What is an Apprenticeship

An apprenticeship is a work-based training program that allows individuals to gain practical experience and theoretical knowledge in a particular field while being paid a wage. Apprenticeships can range from entry-level positions to higher-level roles that require more specialized skills and knowledge. They are usually offered in industries such as construction, engineering, healthcare, and hospitality, among others.

Positives

Gain practical, on-the-job experience in a chosen field
Earn a wage while learning new skills and gaining qualifications
Opportunity to work towards a nationally recognized qualification, such as a BTEC or NVQ
Develop transferable skills such as teamwork, communication, and problem-solving
Potential for career progression within the company or industry

Negatives

May receive a lower wage than other entry-level roles
May have limited opportunities for socializing or meeting peers outside of work
May not receive the same level of training as a traditional college or university course
May have limited flexibility with regards to working hours or shifts
May face a competitive job market upon completion of the apprenticeship
